Classic Homemade Buttermilk Biscuits

Whip up a batch of these delicious homemade buttermilk biscuits as a tasty base for breakfast or a great side dish for any of your favorite meals!

PREP TIME

45min

SERVINGS

12 servings

Instructions

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper then lightly butter the parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, baking powder, and baking soda until combined.
  3. Add the shortening to the flour mixture and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to blend until coarse crumbs form.
  4. Next, grate the frozen butter into the flour mixture and stir until all the butter pieces are coated in flour.
  5. Add the buttermilk and stir until the mixture becomes a sticky dough.
  6. Lightly sprinkle flour onto a clean work surface, then pour the dough onto the surface and pat down into a ½ inch thick rectangle.
  7. Sprinkle the top with a tablespoon of flour, then fold the dough into thirds.
  8. Repeat this process, rotating the dough 90 degrees each time, for a total of three times.
  9. Then, roll or pat out the dough until it is ¾ inch thick and no longer sticky on top.
  10. Dip the cookie cutter or glass in flour, then cut out the biscuits and transfer them to the prepared baking sheet.
  11. Pat out the scraps to ¾ inch again and cut out more biscuits until no dough remains.
  12. Refrigerate the biscuits for 15 minutes.
  13. Preheat the oven to 450°F.
  14. Then, bake for 12 to 15 minutes until golden brown and crisp on top.
  15. Let stand for 5 minutes before serving. If desired, brush the tops with melted butter as soon as they are removed from the oven.

Ingredients

8 tablespoons unsalted butter, frozen
2 ½ cups all-purpose flour, spooned and leveled
1 tablespoon baking powder
1 teaspoon sugar
1 teaspoon salt
½ teaspoon baking soda
2 tablespoons vegetable shortening
1 ½ cups buttermilk
¼ cup all-purpose flour, as needed
