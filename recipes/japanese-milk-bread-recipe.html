Homemade Japanese Milk Bread

You will fall in love with this deliciously soft and slightly sweet homemade Japanese milk bread recipe!

PREP TIME

4h 10min

SERVINGS

9 servings

Instructions

  1. In a small saucepan, combine 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour with 6 tablespoons of milk. Cook over low heat for 2 minutes, stirring constantly. It will become a thickened paste.
  2. Remove from the saucepan and pour into a small bowl to let cool.
  3. In a separate large bowl, whisk together the bread flour and salt.
  4. Make a well in the center of the flour and place the sugar and yeast into the well.
  5. Pour the cream into the yeast and sugar and whisk until the sugar dissolves, being careful to not pull in any of the flour from the sides, but just whisk the cream with sugar and yeast.
  6. Whisk together one egg with the milk and flour paste.
  7. To the flour, sugar, and yeast mixture, add the egg and paste mixture and then the melted butter. Stir to combine and create a dough.
  8. Once mostly combined, use a clean hand to knead the dough in the bowl until smooth, turning it back over itself, then pushing down and repeating the process.
  9. When the dough is smooth, cover loosely and set aside in a warm place for 1 hour to double in size.
  10. Once the dough has risen, divide it into 9 pieces and roll each portion into a ball.
  11. On a clean surface, roll each ball into a rope approximately 8" to 10" long and slightly less than ½" in diameter.
  12. Carefully twist the rope of dough into a knot by creating a loop and pulling the ends through it.
  13. Grease or line a 9" round baking dish with parchment paper.
  14. Place the dough knots into the prepared pan so that the sides are touching.
  15. Set aside for 30-minutes to rise again.
  16. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  17. Whisk together 1 egg with 1 tablespoon water then brush the egg wash over the tops of the dough knots.
  18. Bake for 30-minutes until golden brown on top.
  19. Serve hot with butter or jam.

Ingredients

2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
6 tablespoons milk
½ teaspoon salt
2 ½ cups bread flour
2 teaspoons active dry yeast
2 tablespoons sugar
½ cup heavy cream
¼ cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
1 egg beaten
1 egg
1 tablespoon water
